Welcome To Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery!

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, located on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, is a haven for the conservation and protection of sea turtles. This remarkable facility plays a vital role in preserving these endangered creatures and educating the public about their significance. The hatchery works tirelessly to rescue turtle eggs from vulnerable nesting sites and provide a safe environment for their incubation. Once the eggs hatch, the hatchery nurtures the baby turtles until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. Visitors to the hatchery can witness this incredible process firsthand, learning about the different species of turtles and the challenges they face in the wild. By promoting awareness and sustainable practices, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery serves as a beacon of hope for the future of these magnificent creatures and the preservation of our oceans.

Turtle Species Surrounding Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa, a picturesque coastal town in Sri Lanka, is blessed with the presence of various sea turtle species. The area is particularly known for its abundance of Green Turtles (Chelonia mydas), which can often be seen gracefully swimming through the crystal-clear waters. These magnificent creatures are characterized by their olive-colored shells and herbivorous diet. Another commonly spotted species is the Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata), distinguished by its stunning patterned shell and sharp, curving beak. Loggerhead Turtles (Caretta caretta), with their robust heads and strong jaws, also make appearances along the shores of Hikkaduwa. Occasionally, lucky visitors may even catch a glimpse of the enormous Leatherback Turtles (Dermochelys coriacea), the largest sea turtle species, gliding through the waves. The presence of these diverse sea turtle species is a testament to the vibrant marine ecosystem that thrives in Hikkaduwa, emphasizing the need for their conservation and protection.

Green turtle

The Green Sea Turtle, found in Hikkaduwa and oceans worldwide, is an iconic species known for its olive-colored carapace. As herbivores, they contribute to marine ecosystems by feeding on seagrass and algae. These gentle giants can grow up to four feet long and weigh over 400 pounds. With graceful swimming abilities, they navigate the waters effortlessly. However, Green Sea Turtles face various challenges like habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching. Conservation efforts are vital to protect these magnificent creatures and ensure the health of our oceans.

Hawksbill Turtle

In Hikkaduwa, the vibrant coral reefs attract Hawksbill Turtles, providing them with food and protection. These turtles feed on sponges, algae, and marine invertebrates, contributing to the ecosystem's delicate balance. Hikkaduwa is a significant nesting site for Hawksbill Turtles, and they prefer the sandy beaches for breeding. The presence of these turtles highlights the importance of conserving and safeguarding their habitat in Hikkaduwa.

Loggerhead Turtle

Loggerhead sea turtles are large and powerful creatures found in coastal regions worldwide. They prefer warm waters and often visit tropical and subtropical areas. Their diet mainly consists of bottom-dwelling invertebrates like crabs, mollusks, and jellyfish. These turtles are impressive in size, with adult females growing up to three feet long and weighing hundreds of pounds. They undertake incredible migration journeys, spanning vast distances across oceans to reach their nesting beaches. Their nesting sites are carefully chosen and often return to the same beach where they were born. Despite their remarkable traits, Loggerhead sea turtles face numerous threats and conservation efforts are crucial to ensure their survival for future generations.

Leatherback Turtle

The leatherback sea turtle is a remarkable and unique species found in oceans worldwide. It is the largest of all sea turtles, reaching lengths of up to seven feet and weighing over a thousand pounds. Unlike other turtles, the leatherback's shell is flexible and covered in a leathery skin. They are skilled divers, capable of diving to great depths to hunt jellyfish, squid, and sea urchins. With their wide-ranging habitat, leatherback sea turtles migrate between foraging grounds and nesting beaches, covering vast distances. Preserving their habitats and raising awareness about their conservation is essential for the survival of these extraordinary creatures.
