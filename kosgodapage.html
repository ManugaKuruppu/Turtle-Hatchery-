Welcome To The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery Website!

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a remarkable conservation center located in the beautiful coastal village of Kosgoda, Sri Lanka. Known for its dedication to the preservation and protection of sea turtles, the hatchery plays a vital role in ensuring the survival of these magnificent creatures. The center actively participates in turtle conservation efforts by collecting eggs laid on nearby beaches, safeguarding them from natural and human threats, and providing a safe environment for their hatching. Once the baby turtles emerge, visitors can witness the magical sight of these tiny hatchlings making their way to the ocean, beginning their incredible journey of life. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery not only educates and raises awareness about the importance of turtle conservation but also actively contributes to the sustainable growth of the turtle population in the region.

Turtle Species In Kosgoda

OLIVE RIDLEY

The Olive Ridley sea turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea) is a captivating species known for its olive-colored, heart-shaped carapace. They exhibit a unique behavior called "arribadas," where large groups of females gather to nest simultaneously on specific beaches. These turtles are found in tropical and subtropical waters, such as the Indian and Pacific Oceans. Unfortunately, they face significant threats from habitat degradation, fishing, and pollution. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect and preserve these ancient creatures and ensure their survival for future generations to appreciate their mesmerizing beauty.

GREEN TURTLE

The Green sea turtle (Chelonia mydas) is a captivating species found in tropical and subtropical waters worldwide. Named for its distinctive green color, these turtles are cherished by conservationists and nature enthusiasts. With a herbivorous diet centered around seagrass and algae, they contribute to the health of marine ecosystems. However, the Green sea turtle faces numerous threats, including habitat loss, pollution, and illegal hunting. Conservation efforts are crucial to safeguard their existence and preserve their remarkable beauty for future generations.

LOGGERHEAD

The Loggerhead sea turtle (Caretta caretta) is a remarkable and robust species known for its reddish-brown carapace. They are the largest hard-shelled turtles in the world and can be found in various oceans. Loggerheads have a diverse diet and play an important role in marine ecosystems. However, they face threats such as habitat destruction and pollution. Conservation efforts are crucial to safeguard the loggerhead sea turtle and appreciate their significance in maintaining marine biodiversity.

LEATHERBACK

The leatherback sea turtle is a fascinating and unique species known for its large size, leathery shell, and impressive migratory abilities. They can grow up to 6 to 8 feet in length and weigh over 1,000 pounds. These turtles have a black or dark gray carapace with white or pale pink spots. They are remarkable divers, descending to great depths to feed on jellyfish. However, they face numerous threats, including habitat loss, pollution, climate change, and entanglement in fishing gear. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect these ancient creatures and ensure their survival in the future.

HAWKSBILL

The hawksbill sea turtle is a critically endangered species known for its distinctive beak-like bill and colorful shell. They are relatively small, reaching lengths of 2 to 3 feet. Hawksbills primarily feed on sponges and play a crucial role in coral reef ecosystems. However, they face threats such as habitat degradation, poaching, accidental capture, and climate change. Conservation efforts are vital to protect these remarkable turtles and their habitats.
