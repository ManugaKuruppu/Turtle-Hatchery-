Welcome To The Benthota Turtle Hatchery Website!

Bentota Turtle Hatchery, located on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, is a renowned conservation project dedicated to protecting endangered sea turtles. This vital sanctuary serves as a safe haven for these remarkable creatures, ensuring their survival in an increasingly challenging environment. At the hatchery, visitors can witness the awe-inspiring process of turtle nesting, egg incubation, and the release of hatchlings into the vast Indian Ocean. The dedicated staff and volunteers work tirelessly to educate the public about the importance of turtle conservation, emphasizing the need to preserve their nesting grounds and combat threats such as habitat loss and poaching. The Bentota Turtle Hatchery stands as a beacon of hope, striving to safeguard these gentle marine reptiles for future generations to cherish and admire.

Turtle Species In Benthota

Bentota, situated on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, is home to a diverse range of turtle species that grace its pristine shores. One of the most commonly found species is the Olive Ridley turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea), known for its olive-colored carapace and a penchant for nesting in large groups. The Green turtle (Chelonia mydas) is another prominent resident, recognized for its beautiful greenish-brown shell and herbivorous diet. Hawksbill turtles (Eretmochelys imbricata), with their strikingly patterned shells, are also spotted along the shores of Bentota. Loggerhead turtles (Caretta caretta) occasionally visit these waters, showcasing their distinct reddish-brown carapaces. These magnificent turtle species in Bentota serve as a testament to the area's importance as a vital nesting and feeding ground, further highlighting the significance of conservation efforts in preserving their fragile habitats.
